# Project Description

The Dream of Ball is an experimental reinterpretation of Pong that explores what happens when players experience the game from the perspective of the ball instead of the paddle.

The project investigates the research question:

**What happens when players experience Pong from the perspective of the ball?**

The experience is structured as four dream-like acts. In each act, the ball gradually takes on a different role within the game system, moving from being a passive object to becoming the paddle, the game boundary, and finally attempting to escape into the physical world.

Through user testing, I discovered that changing the player's identity can create emotional attachment toward objects that are normally ignored. Players gradually began to treat the ball as a character rather than a simple game object.

The project also revealed that better performance does not necessarily create better experiences. While keyboard controls produced higher scores, the physical controller and motion-based interaction created a stronger sense of immersion and playfulness.

Through making, testing and reflecting, I learned how interaction design can influence the relationship between people and objects, and how gameplay mechanics can encourage empathy toward non-human entities.
