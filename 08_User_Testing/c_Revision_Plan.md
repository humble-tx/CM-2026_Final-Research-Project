# Revision Plan

If more time were available, I would continue improving both the physical controller and the narrative experience.

The current prototype relies on breadboards and jumper wires, which often cause unstable connections. In future versions, I would replace these temporary components with soldered circuit boards and more compact modules. I would also redesign the battery system to improve reliability and study how commercial controllers organise their internal components to achieve a smaller and more robust design.

The IMU and electromagnet systems also require further refinement. More stable power management and better sensor integration would help create more consistent haptic feedback.

Several players eventually started treating the ball as a living character. This feedback inspired me to think beyond the current ending. In future iterations, I would like to explore autonomous movement by placing motor-driven wheels inside the ball. This could allow the ball to move by itself, shift its centre of gravity, and provide physical feedback directly through its own motion.

Rather than simply being controlled by the player, the ball could gradually become an independent character with its own behaviours.

Through this project, I learned that improving performance is not always the same as improving experience, and this idea would continue guiding future development.
